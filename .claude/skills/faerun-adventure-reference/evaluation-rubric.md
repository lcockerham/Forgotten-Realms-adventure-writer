# Adventure Evaluation Rubric

Evaluate one-page adventures across four dimensions. Each dimension scored 1-5.

## 1. Story Coherence and Engagement (1-5)

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Clear, compelling hook that immediately engages
- Logical progression from hook through encounters to resolution
- Multiple paths or player agency moments
- Satisfying conclusion with meaningful choices
- Tight narrative with no plot holes
- Stakes are clear and matter to characters

**Score 4 - Good**
- Strong hook that motivates action
- Encounters flow logically
- Resolution makes sense
- Minor pacing issues or small gaps
- Stakes are present but could be stronger

**Score 3 - Adequate**
- Hook is present but not compelling
- Encounters connect but feel formulaic
- Resolution is predictable or weak
- Some logical gaps in the story
- Stakes are unclear or low

**Score 2 - Weak**
- Hook is confusing or generic
- Encounters feel disconnected
- Resolution is unsatisfying or missing
- Significant plot holes
- No clear stakes

**Score 1 - Poor**
- No clear hook or motivation
- Random encounters with no connection
- No resolution or incoherent ending
- Story makes no sense

### Key Questions
- Why should characters care?
- Do encounters build toward something?
- Is there a satisfying payoff?
- Are there interesting choices?

## 2. Faerun Lore Accuracy (1-5)

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Specific, accurate references to Faerun locations
- Correct use of deity domains and themes
- Appropriate faction involvement with accurate motivations
- Cultural details match the region (language, customs)
- Monster choices fit the setting
- Feels authentically Faerun, not generic fantasy

**Score 4 - Good**
- Accurate major lore elements (deities, factions, locations)
- Minor cultural details present
- Monsters appropriate to region
- Small inaccuracies that don't break immersion

**Score 3 - Adequate**
- Basic lore is correct (deity names, major cities)
- Generic Faerun feel without specific details
- No major lore errors but lacks depth
- Could be set anywhere in Faerun

**Score 2 - Weak**
- Multiple lore inaccuracies
- Factions used incorrectly (wrong motivations)
- Deities outside their domains
- Feels generic, not specific to Faerun

**Score 1 - Poor**
- Major lore violations
- Contradicts established canon
- Wrong deity domains entirely
- Could be any fantasy setting

### Key Questions
- Are deities used according to their domains?
- Do factions act consistently with their goals?
- Is the location specific and accurate?
- Do cultural details fit the region?
- Are monster choices appropriate to Faerun?

## 3. Mechanical Soundness (1-5)

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- All DCs appropriate for level
- Damage values balanced (challenging but not lethal)
- Encounters use action economy well
- Clear mechanical instructions for running
- Trap/hazard mechanics follow 5e standards
- Reward appropriate to challenge
- No rules ambiguity

**Score 4 - Good**
- DCs mostly appropriate with 1-2 minor issues
- Damage balanced overall
- Mechanics clear and usable
- Small rule interpretation needed
- Rewards reasonable

**Score 3 - Adequate**
- DCs inconsistent (some too easy/hard)
- Damage values need adjustment
- Some mechanical ambiguity
- Encounters might need rebalancing
- Rewards not specified or inappropriate

**Score 2 - Weak**
- Multiple DCs wrong for level
- Damage too high or too low consistently
- Confusing or contradictory mechanics
- Encounters likely unbalanced
- Missing critical mechanical information

**Score 1 - Poor**
- Unrunnable without major revision
- Deadly encounters at low level or trivial at high level
- No clear mechanics provided
- Violates 5e rules significantly

### Key Questions
- Are DCs in the right range for the level?
- Would this TPK or bore the party?
- Can a DM run this without confusion?
- Do saves, DCs, and damage follow 5e standards?
- Is the encounter balanced for the stated level?

### Reference DCs
- Level 1-4: Easy 10, Medium 13, Hard 15
- Level 5-10: Easy 12, Medium 15, Hard 18
- Level 11-16: Easy 14, Medium 17, Hard 20
- Level 17-20: Easy 16, Medium 19, Hard 22

### Reference Damage (Traps/Hazards)
- Level 1: 1d6-1d8 (2d6 deadly)
- Level 2-4: 2d6-2d8 (4d6 deadly)
- Level 5-10: 4d6-4d10 (8d6 deadly)
- Level 11+: Scale up

## 4. Format and Usability (1-5)

**Score 5 - Excellent**
- Fits on one page comfortably
- Information organized for quick reference at table
- Clear section breaks and headers
- Room descriptions easy to find and read
- Mechanics inline and unobtrusive
- No unnecessary detail; every word counts
- Map labels match text perfectly
- Can be run with minimal prep

**Score 4 - Good**
- Fits on one page with minor overflow
- Well organized with clear sections
- Easy to run with quick read-through
- Map mostly matches text
- Minor formatting improvements possible

**Score 3 - Adequate**
- Just over one page or cramped
- Organization okay but could improve
- Needs more careful read to run
- Some map/text mismatches
- Some wordiness or unclear descriptions

**Score 2 - Weak**
- Too long for one page
- Poor organization, hard to find information
- Confusing room descriptions
- Map doesn't match text well
- Requires significant prep to run

**Score 1 - Poor**
- Multiple pages or impossibly cramped
- No clear organization
- Unrunnable without major restructuring
- Map useless or missing
- Excessive detail or missing key information

### Key Questions
- Can I read this once and run it?
- Is information where I expect it?
- Do map labels match room descriptions?
- Is it concise without sacrificing clarity?
- Could I run this at a table without prep?

---

## Overall Score Calculation

**Average the four dimension scores:**
- 4.5-5.0: Publication quality, matches Adventures in Faerun standard
- 3.5-4.4: Good, usable with minor tweaks
- 2.5-3.4: Needs revision, has potential
- 1.5-2.4: Significant problems, major revision needed
- 1.0-1.4: Unusable, start over

## Comparative Standard

**"The Curse on Humble Hill" would score:**
- Story: 4-5 (clear hook, logical flow, satisfying multi-part resolution)
- Lore: 5 (Chauntea used correctly, Lords of Waterdeep referenced, specific location)
- Mechanics: 5 (DC 13 appropriate for level 1, 2d6 acid damage balanced, clear monster types)
- Usability: 5 (fits on one page, clear sections, map integrated)
- **Overall: 4.5-5.0** - Publication quality

Use this as the benchmark for generated adventures.
